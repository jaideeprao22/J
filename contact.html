Where I'm useful

What I'm most interested in.

I'm not equally interested in everything that comes under "digital health" or "public health." The list below is roughly the spectrum of work where I add the most, in rough priority order.

Priority 1

Digital health behaviour research

Cyberchondria, problematic digital use, AI-assisted health information seeking, screening-instrument development. Especially in medical-student and young-adult populations.

Priority 2

Instrument validation studies

Cross-cultural validation, adaptation, and psychometric work on digital-behaviour screening instruments for Indian populations — particularly where existing scales were validated only in Western or East Asian samples.

Priority 3

Behavioural epidemiology

Longitudinal cohort work, dose-response modelling, behavioural predictors of health outcomes, instrument validation. Comfortable across cross-sectional and prospective designs.

Priority 4

Aging research in LMIC settings

Nutritional status, biological-aging biomarkers, functional age composites, household-level assessment in rural and semi-urban Indian populations.

Priority 5

Systematic reviews & meta-analysis

Pre-registered (PROSPERO/OSF), dual-reviewer, GRADE-rated, with R-based proportion or effect meta-analysis. Particularly in digital health and medical-student populations.

Priority 6

Student supervision & mentoring

MBBS or postgraduate students working on dissertation-level public-health research, especially in digital health, cyberchondria, or NCD epidemiology.
